FGTS — Severance Fund Anticipation
Recurrence & One Click · Market Saturation — Q2 2025 to Q1 2026
Most recent study: Q1 2026 · N=1,134

What is the product

FGTS Saque-Aniversário allows CLT workers to anticipate their severance fund balance, receiving cash immediately in exchange for an interest rate. It directly competes with credit cards and personal loans.

How it works in the user's life

  • User feels immediate financial pressure (overdue bills, emergency)
  • Searches official sources (Caixa, FGTS app) then their bank's app
  • Hiring is 100% digital — bank app is the main channel (56%)
  • High post-hiring satisfaction (83%) but loyalty is conditional on price

Market dynamics

  • Saturated market: top 10 institutions capture ~80% of transactions
  • Caixa dominates first hire; Nubank leads in recurrence
  • Inter and Meu Tudo growing fast among recurring Mass Market users
  • ~40% of Nubank clients switch banks on the second hire

Private Payroll — Consignado Privado
Drop & Success Cases · Market Deep Dive — Q2 2025 to Q4 2025
Most recent study: Q4 2025 · Qualitative + Deep Dive

What is the product

Private payroll loan with direct paycheck deduction for CLT private sector workers. Objectively safer and cheaper than personal loans, but suffers from structural invisibility — most eligibles don't know the product exists at Nubank.

The awareness problem

  • 88% of eligibles have never had a Private Payroll offer at Nubank
  • 8 in 10 eligibles never knew Nubank offered the product
  • Discovery is accidental: app notification, TikTok, word of mouth
  • 54% are CLT but only 19% are active product users

Eligible user context

  • 88% of eligibles haven't taken any loan in the last 6 months
  • 59% declare Nubank as primary bank; 58% would take a loan at Nu if needed
  • Caixa (46–56%) and Itaú (31–33%) are the main competitors with accounts
  • Personal loan is the mental benchmark — everything is compared to it

The journey in 4 stages

  • Awareness: accidental discovery, superficial understanding. User ignores technical rules and risks
  • Consideration: driven by stability and security. Trust and installment amount are decisive criteria
  • Conversion: dictated by monthly installment affordability, trust, and smooth experience
  • Post-hiring: most fragmented and negative stage. Contract insecurity, portability off radar

Eligibles have a lower-risk profile — financially organized, no immediate credit need, negative view of loans. Users are mostly CLT in urgency. This gap explains why awareness doesn't automatically convert into hiring.

Nuzaré Wave 3 · N=571 users / 426 eligibles

46% of users simulated other loans at Nubank before hiring PP — 41% simulated PL and 38% simulated FGTS. Main reason for choosing PP was lower rate (39%), followed by easier installments (28%). PP wins on logic, but loses on familiarity.

Nuzaré Wave 3 · N=571 users

Market Deep Dive Q4/2025
Consignable margin not understood — no profile understands what margin is, how it's calculated, or why it limits installments. Drives abandonment and trial-and-error in the calculator.
Unknown product vs. personal loan — PL is the mental benchmark. Payroll loan feels "more complicated" even though it's objectively better. The battle is about familiarity, not price.
Drop is exploratory, not rejection — much of the abandonment is curiosity or comparison. The user leaves to compare and may not return.
Weak post-hiring journey across the market — competitors don't deliver clarity, follow-up, or support after hiring. Opportunity to differentiate with better contract management.
Portability, refinancing, and renegotiation treated as synonyms — terms presented without conceptual explanation. Users don't know the difference and feel insecure acting on them.

INSS — Payroll Loan for Retirees
INSS X-Ray · Top-up Research · Assistance Benchmarking · Nuzaré Wave 3 — Q3 2025 to Q1 2026

What is the product

Payroll loan for INSS retirees and pensioners, with automatic deduction from the benefit. The most accessible credit product for an audience with limited access to the traditional financial system.

Audience-specific challenges

  • ~30% of those 60+ don't own a smartphone
  • 6 in 10 with low digital literacy have no internet access
  • Physical branch is the main hiring channel (78%)
  • Nubank still seen with distrust (20–26% give score 0 on safety)

Top-up dynamics

  • 9 in 10 clients prefer portability with Top-up vs. without
  • Ideal offer: lower installment + longer term + Top-up (even if it raises total cost)
  • 34% would only do portability if Top-up is available
  • Top-up awareness grows with experience: 77% of those with 5+ contracts know it

Digital and access barriers — 30% of 60+ have no smartphone. Among those who do, many lack confidence (44% of 71+ say "not at all confident"). The app is the barrier before the product.
Rates and interest not understood — users can't translate percentage into real impact on their benefit. The simulation shows numbers, not practical consequences.
In-app support perceived as insufficient bot — chat is seen as automated and unable to resolve sensitive credit doubts. Users need a human voice for loan contracts.
Invisible post-hiring — automatic benefit deduction means most users don't monitor the contract. When they return to the app, they find no clear management, history, or end-of-contract alerts.
Structural distrust of Nubank — for 71+, Nubank doesn't carry the "weight" of a traditional bank. Physical presence, branch manager, and printed contract are missing.
Non-PBRs don't understand how Nubank loans work — 23% of Non-PBRs cite this as reason to hire elsewhere, vs. just 2% of PBRs. It's a communication barrier, not a product one.

Public Payroll — Consignado Público
Public X-Ray · SIAPE Deep Dive · Community Benchmarking — Q4 2024 to Q1 2026
Most recent study: Q1 2026 · N=955 + Qualitative

What is the product

Payroll loan for municipal, state, federal, and military public servants. Stable, predictable income audience but strongly anchored to traditional banks with preference for physical and human channels.

Profiles and dynamics by sphere

  • Municipal: lower income (66% earn up to 3 min. wages), higher credit appetite
  • State: more open to digital within the bank where they receive their salary
  • Federal: higher income, more investments, less credit pressure
  • Military: young profile, more open to Nubank, but not to loan hiring

Journey particularities

  • Multi-channel journey: bank app + SouGov/E-Consig + pay stub + branch manager
  • User doesn't expect centralization — expects predictability of where to go
  • Loan agent is a relevant intermediary, especially for military
  • Contract management happens outside the app (pay stub, SouGov)

There are four distinct SIAPE profiles: PBR who hires at Nu (agility as escape from harassment), Non-PBR who hires at Nu (autonomy), Non-PBR who hires at competitor (rate comparison), and PBR who uses Nu daily but hires elsewhere (daily UX vs. safety for large amounts).

SIAPE Deep Dive Q1/2026

"Mental accounting" is the invisible barrier: users separate "everyday money" (Nubank — agility) from "serious money" (traditional bank — stability). Nubank is still not seen as a long-term structural partner.

SIAPE Deep Dive Q1/2026

Children and relatives are the main conversion vector: 82% of servants up to 44 years have children/relatives who use Nubank, creating a natural recommendation channel for the credit product.

Public X-Ray Q4/2025 · N=955

Human channel is not a substitute for digital — it's a strategic complement. Managers, loan agents, and WhatsApp are triggered when they save time, translate risk, or unlock app gaps. Civilians and military use them for different reasons, but both do.

Community Benchmarking Q1/2026
The security paradox — Nubank's smooth UX becomes a disadvantage for high-value contracts. "Too easy" triggers fear of fraud or error. A branch manager's friction is reinterpreted as a guarantee of seriousness.
Rate as a penny dealbreaker — a PBR who uses Nubank for everything hires payroll at BB for a 0.06% rate difference. "Nubank was at 1.95% and BB at 1.89% — that was enough for me to walk away." UX doesn't compensate for perceived mathematical disadvantage.
Portability, refinancing, and renegotiation confused — actions presented without conceptual explanation. When users don't find them quickly, they conclude "it doesn't exist in the app" and go to human channels.
Harassment from loan agents and fintechs — 50%+ report being bothered by excessive offers via WhatsApp/SMS/calls. They associate invasive approaches with small companies, not large banks.
Fragmented post-hiring management — monitoring happens via pay stub, SouGov, and bank app in parallel. No single source of truth. When information diverges, insecurity grows.
